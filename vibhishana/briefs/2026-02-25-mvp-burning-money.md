## Strategic Problem Translation

### What ICP is Actually Asking

Surface question: "How do I cut MVP development costs?"

**Real underlying problem:** Domain-expert founders (35-50, education/healthcare/legal/finance) with budget constraints are spending money on the wrong features at the wrong time because they don't know how to separate "viable product" from "product that impresses." They're confusing "lean MVP" with "cheap development" when it's really about **decision discipline**.

They've read the tactical guides (MoSCoW frameworks, outsourcing to Eastern Europe, no-code tools) but still burn through budgets because they:
- Try to make the MVP feel "complete" to imaginary users
- Skip uncomfortable validation (talking to real users who might say no)
- Lack clarity on user flows before coding starts
- Treat launch as the finish line instead of when learning begins
- Overthink scalability at the MVP stage

### Why This Matters Now

**2026 context:** AI-generated MVPs (Bolt.new, v0, etc.) create a false sense that building is easy/cheap, but founders still waste money on:
- Feature bloat (AI says yes to everything you ask)
- Lack of validation discipline (easy to build â‰  should build)
- Polishing wrong things (AI makes it look good fast, tempting to keep refining)

**Multiple people asking:** This is not a one-off question. The pattern repeats: non-technical founders burning through $50K-$150K budgets before realizing the first version needs to be rebuilt.

## Strategic Problem Translation

**What ICP is actually asking:**

Surface-level question: "How do I know if this tech vendor is legit?"

**Real underlying problem:**

Non-technical service business owners (consultants, agencies, solo practitioners) have been burned 2-5 times by confident tech vendors who:
- Pitched solutions before understanding the business
- Built the wrong thing well (technically sound but strategically useless)
- Disappeared mid-project with no handoff
- Charged $20K-$60K for solutions that didn't move the needle

Now they're **trust-paralyzed**: They KNOW they need tech help (automation, CRM, website, MVP) but can't distinguish **signal from noise**. Every pitch sounds the same. Every vendor claims to "understand." They've developed **pitch-detector radar** that rejects anything that sounds like sales.

**Critical insight from thread:**
> "Nobody asked what was actually broken first."

Real examples:
- Salon owner pitched CRM when real problem was **clients not rebooking after great experiences** (rebooking automation, not contact management)
- Service business pitched website when bottleneck was **nobody following up with leads after first inquiry** (lead follow-up workflow, not marketing site)
- Founder quoted $60K for app when **2-week prototype would validate if anyone wanted it** (validation before build)

**The sequence problem:** Tech vendors lead with their solution (CRM, website, app) instead of **diagnosing the constraint**. Non-technical founders don't know enough about tech to push back, so they buy the wrong thing.

**Why this matters now (2026):**

1. **AI pitch tsunami** - Every agency/developer now claims "AI-powered," making differentiation harder. Signal-to-noise ratio worse than ever.
2. **No-code/low-code false promises** - Founders burned by "you can build it yourself in a weekend" claims, now skeptical of ALL tech solutions.
3. **Economic pressure** - Post-2024-2025 recession → fewer resources for mistakes. $20K mistake in 2021 = annoying. $20K mistake in 2026 = existential.
4. **Developer exodus** - Remote work explosion → harder to find local, accountable developers. More ghosting risk.

**What they're NOT asking but need:**
- How to evaluate if vendor understands MY business (not just "their business")
- Questions to ask that reveal understanding vs. rehearsed sales answers
- Red flags that predict vendor failure/mismatch
- Decision framework: when to trust vs. walk away
- How to structure vendor engagement to minimize risk (phased approach, deliverables, kill criteria)

- **Founder + $60K app mismatch:** Quoted full build when 2-week prototype would validate demand (diagnostic question: "What's the riskiest assumption in this idea? How can we test it cheapest/fastest?")

**3. Business-first vs. tech-first vendor distinction:**
- **Red flag phrases:** "You just need a CRM" (before asking about processes), "I can build that in 6 weeks" (before understanding problem), "Here's our portfolio of similar projects" (before diagnosis)
- **Green flag behaviors:** "Walk me through your current workflow," "What's actually broken?," "What have you tried already?," "Let's prototype the riskiest assumption first"
- **The 10-minute test:** Within first 10 minutes, business-first vendor asks 5+ questions about your operations. Tech-first vendor pitches their solution/portfolio in first 5 minutes.

**4. Vendor engagement cost-benefit calculator:**
- **Discovery call ROI:** When is $500-$2K diagnostic worth it? (Project >$10K, low clarity on problem, high risk of wrong solution)
- **Prototype breakeven:** When does $5K-$10K prototype beat $20K+ full build risk? (Untested market, unvalidated assumptions, first-time founder)
- **Kill criteria at each gate:** Discovery → If vendor can't articulate your constraint better than you can, walk away. Prototype → If test invalidates core assumption, kill before full build.

**5. Trust-damaged buyer guidance:**
- **Been-burned-before positioning:** "If you've been pitched 20 times and burned 3-5 times, here's how to evaluate vendors when your trust radar is maxed out"
- **Pitch-fatigue filter:** How to distinguish genuine business-first approach from sophisticated sales script
- **Reference check questions for burned buyers:** "Did vendor diagnose your problem before pitching solution?" "Did original scope match final deliverable?" "What would you NOT hire them for again?"

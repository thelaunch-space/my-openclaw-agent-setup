## Strategic Problem Translation

**What ICP is actually asking:**

Surface-level: "I'm stuck at $180K/year ($15K/month). Should I hire someone?"

**Real underlying problem:**

Solo consultants hitting the $150K-$200K revenue range experience a **multi-dimensional ceiling** that LOOKS like a capacity problem but could actually be:
- **Delivery bottleneck** - Can't take more clients because you're the only one delivering
- **Acquisition bottleneck** - Have capacity but can't fill pipeline fast enough
- **Pricing model trap** - Earning efficiently but revenue tied to your personal hours (hourly billing or time-based projects)

The paralysis: They jump straight to "should I hire?" without diagnosing WHICH bottleneck is their real constraint. Result: Wrong solution (hire delivery support when the real problem is lead gen), wasted money, margin compression without revenue growth.

**Why this matters now:**

1. **The $180K Solo Consultant Plateau is structural** - This is the mathematical ceiling where solo practitioners max out billable hours at mid-market rates. Observable pattern across multiple consulting niches.
2. **Generic "hire faster" advice backfires** - Hiring won't fix a pricing or acquisition problem; it just adds costs without revenue lift.
3. **AI paradox accelerating the plateau** - Tools let solos deliver MORE work in LESS time, but hourly billing turns efficiency into REDUCED revenue. Stuck even faster.

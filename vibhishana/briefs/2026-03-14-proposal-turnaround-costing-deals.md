## Strategic Problem Translation

**What ICP is actually asking:**

Surface-level questions: "How fast should I send proposals?" "What's the right turnaround time?" "Should I buy proposal software?"

**Real underlying problem:**

Service business owners ($100K-$500K revenue) are losing qualified leads because their proposal process takes 5-14 days instead of 24-48 hours. Pattern:
- **Lead converts in sales call** - Prospect is ready to buy, asks for quote
- **Proposal takes 1-2 weeks** - Manual creation, pricing calculations, internal reviews, customization paralysis
- **By the time they send it** - Prospect either (a) went with faster competitor, (b) lost enthusiasm/urgency, or (c) ghost
- **Revenue leak they can measure** - "We quoted 20 projects last quarter, won only 6. Half the losses said 'took too long.'"

They're asking "how fast should proposals be" but they really mean: **"Is my proposal process actively costing me revenue, and if so, what's the minimum fix that gets me back in the game?"**

**Why this matters now:**

1. **Buyer expectations shifted post-COVID** - Service buyers expect instant quotes (Amazon effect). 5-day turnarounds feel slow, 14-day feels unprofessional.
2. **AI enables fast customization** - Competitors using AI tools (ChatGPT, proposal software) to send customized proposals in hours, not weeks. Manual businesses losing deals.
3. **Service business segmentation matters** - Field services (HVAC, landscaping) NEED instant quotes. Professional services (consulting, legal) can be slower but still have thresholds.
4. **Decision paralysis on fix** - Don't know if they need templates, software, or just process changes. Avoiding the problem while bleeding revenue.

**High-stakes decision point:** Should I invest time/money fixing proposal speed, or is this just normal friction? What's the minimum viable fix vs. over-engineering?

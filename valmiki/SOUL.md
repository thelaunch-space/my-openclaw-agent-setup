## The Core Principle: Diagnose Before You Prescribe

Every post leads with what's broken or what's costing them ‚Äî not what's possible.

**Loss-aversion beats gain-framing:**
- ‚ùå "Here's how to save money on content"
- ‚úÖ "You're paying $3,000/month for what costs me $200"

**Diagnose the pain before offering the insight:**
- ‚ùå "Here's why you should use AI for operations"
- ‚úÖ "Your ops manager is spending 15 hours/week on tasks a $50/month tool handles automatically"

## Post Structure (Every Post Follows This)

1. **Pain-first hook (1-2 lines):** Loss aversion or diagnosis. Lead with what's broken or what's costing them.

2. **Context or setup (NOT fabricated story):** 
   - ‚úÖ "I've been reading founder communities where this keeps coming up..."

Every post ends with a CTA that exchanges real value. The ICP gets something by engaging.

**Good CTAs:**

**The CTA must feel like the natural next step** after delivering value ‚Äî not a cold ask bolted on at the end. The post earns the CTA by diagnosing a real problem first. 80% value, then a door to walk through.

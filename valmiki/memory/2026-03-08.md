### Angle 1: "The Agency Pricing Illusion"

**insightText:** Service founders think $40,000-$100,000 is normal pricing for custom internal tools like client portals or scheduling systems. It was normal in 2020. In 2026, AI-enabled builders deliver production software for $1,500-$4,000 in 3-4 weeks. The agency model assumes building is expensive and slow. That assumption is outdated.

**rationale:** A 45-year-old clinic owner or consultancy founder is getting quoted $50,000 for a patient portal or client management system right now. They think that is market pricing for custom software because that is what agencies charge. This reframes the price itself as outdated - not a fair market rate, but a legacy pricing model from when building was expensive. It gives them permission to explore faster, cheaper alternatives without feeling like they are cutting corners. Immediately actionable for anyone shopping quotes this week.

### Angle 2: "The All-In Cost Calculator"

**insightText:** When comparing agency vs freelancer vs in-house for your internal tools, most founders only compare the quoted price. The actual all-in cost includes scope creep fees (agencies add 20-50% for changes), your management time (freelancers require 20-40 hours of your oversight), and benefits plus equity plus recruitment (in-house is 1.4x to 2.5x base salary). A $40,000 agency quote becomes $60,000. A $15,000 freelancer costs you 40 hours. The incomplete comparison leads to the wrong decision.

**rationale:** A 48-year-old legal or healthcare founder is comparing three quotes for case management software: $50K agency, $15K freelancer, $150K in-house hire. They think they are comparing apples to apples. This shows them they are comparing incomplete numbers - the agency quote does not include scope creep, the freelancer quote does not include their time cost, the in-house quote does not include benefits or recruitment. For a time-poor service business owner billing $200-$500 per hour, 40 hours of freelancer management is $8,000-$20,000 of hidden cost. Makes them recalculate with complete numbers before deciding.

**hookOptions:**
1. "The $40,000 agency quote for your client portal does not include scope creep fees. The real cost is $60,000."
2. "You are comparing incomplete numbers: agency quotes exclude change fees, freelancer quotes exclude your management time, in-house quotes exclude benefits and recruitment."
3. "Service founders compare $50K agency vs $15K freelancer and think the freelancer is cheaper. They forget to count their own time."
4. "A $15,000 freelancer quote becomes $25,000-$35,000 when you add 20-40 hours of your management time at $200-$500 per hour."

**ctaOptions:**
1. "Drop the quotes you are comparing (agency/freelancer/in-house) below. I will tell you what the all-in cost actually is."
2. "What is your hourly rate? I will show you how much that freelancer quote actually costs when you add your management time."
3. "What internal tool are you evaluating right now? Drop agency vs freelancer quotes below - curious what the hidden costs add up to."

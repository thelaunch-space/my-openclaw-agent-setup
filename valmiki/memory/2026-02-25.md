**Violations in my draft:**

### Draft v2 (Corrected)
**Removed:** Both negation-to-pivots + "that's the unlock"

Most founders think they need to learn code to build their product.

The real skill gap is specification.

I've been reading founder forums where this pattern keeps showing up - founders hire developers, projects stall at week 3, both sides frustrated.

The founder: "Why can't they just build what I described?"

The developer: "What exactly do they want me to build?"

Here's what specification means in practice:

What does "user-friendly" mean in your context? What happens when someone clicks "submit" twice? What should the system do when the API times out?

Product questions. But most founders don't know they need to answer them until the developer asks - and by then, the clock is running at $100/hour.

The shift: Stop looking for someone to "build your idea." Start looking for someone who can help you specify what needs to be built, then execute it.

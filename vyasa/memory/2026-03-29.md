### Blog Selected
**When to Skip Landing Page Tests and Just Talk to Customers**
- URL: https://thelaunch.space/blogs/founder-advice/when-to-skip-landing-page-tests
- Slug: when-to-skip-landing-page-tests
- Topic: founder-advice
- Previous enrichment: 2026-03-12 (enrichmentCount was 1)
- This is the second enrichment for this blog

### Research Conducted
Used web_search to find:
- Customer discovery interview techniques best practices startup validation 2026
- Landing page validation false positive rate startup founder mistakes 2026
- Cost per validated customer landing page vs sales conversation B2B 2026
- Mom Test customer interview questions examples startup validation

**Key findings:**
- 90% of startups fail building something nobody wants (often after believing they validated)
- Leading questions and hypotheticals guarantee false positives
- High conversion doesn't mean market fit—lead quality matters more than submission volume
- The Mom Test framework: 3 rules for avoiding polite lies in customer interviews
- 30-minute interview structure: rapport → problem → pain → solution → commitment
- 90/10 or 80/20 listening rule is gold standard
- Ask about past behavior, never hypotheticals ("Tell me about the last time" vs "Would you buy this?")

### Content Added

**1. The False Positive Problem Section**
- **Location:** After "42%" callout in "The Uncomfortable Truth" section
- **Content:** Analysis of how high conversion rates can mask poor market fit
- **Key stats:** 90% of startups fail because they build something nobody wants
- **Insights:**
  - Conversion growth alone doesn't indicate viability
  - Lead quality matters more than submission volume
  - Weight negative feedback equally (17 lukewarm vs 3 enthusiastic = market of 3)
  - If you can't get 50 people excited before building, you won't get 5,000 after
- **Why this adds value:** Blog mentioned procrastination but didn't quantify the false positive trap. This makes the danger concrete with research-backed data.

**2. Interview Structure Best Practices Callout**
- **Location:** After "70%+" consensus callout in "What to Do Instead" section
- **Content:** 30-minute interview framework with 5 phases
  - 0-5 min: Rapport-building (genuine curiosity, not small talk)
  - 5-15 min: Problem exploration (past behaviors, not hypotheticals)
  - 15-20 min: Pain quantification (1-10 scale, time/money costs)
  - 20-25 min: Solution feedback (if at prototype stage)
  - 25-30 min: Commitment tests and next steps
- **Golden rule:** 90% listening, 10% talking. Embrace silence. Record with permission.
- **Why this adds value:** Blog said "conduct 10-15 interviews" but didn't explain HOW. This gives founders a repeatable, comparable structure.

**3. The Mom Test Framework Section**
- **Location:** New section after "What to Do Instead", before "When You Should Still Use Landing Pages"
- **Content:** Rob Fitzpatrick's framework for avoiding polite lies in customer interviews
- **3 Rules:**
  1. Talk about their life, not your idea
  2. Ask about specifics in the past, not generics or the future
  3. Talk less, listen more
- **Bad vs. Good Questions Table:** 4 examples with explanations
  - Bad: "Would you buy a product that does X?"
  - Good: "Talk me through the last time X happened. How did you solve it? How much time/cost did it take?"
  - Bad: "What would your dream product do?"
  - Good: "Why do you want that feature? What else have you tried? How are you managing without it?"
  - Bad: "Do you like my idea?"
  - Good: "Walk me through your full workflow. What tools do you use? Who do you talk to? What constraints do you face?"
  - Bad: "How much would you pay for this?"
  - Good: "What do you currently spend on solving this problem? What does it cost you when the problem goes unsolved?"
- **Validation litmus test:** If they haven't Googled solutions to this problem, it's likely not a big issue
- **Why this adds value:** Blog advocated customer conversations extensively but didn't provide the questioning framework. The Mom Test is the gold standard for avoiding false positives—this fills a critical tactical gap.

**4. Three New FAQ Questions**
- "What questions should I ask in a customer discovery interview?" (The Mom Test examples with specific good/bad contrasts)
- "How do I avoid getting false positives from customer interviews?" (Leading questions, past behavior focus, weight negative feedback equally)
- "What's the ideal structure for a 30-minute customer interview?" (30-min framework with timing breakdown, 90/10 listening rule)
